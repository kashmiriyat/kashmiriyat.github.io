Khatamband: Cieling art in continuous decline

Khatamband is the art of making a ceiling, by using pieces of wood and fitting them in a geometrical pattern. The whole process is done by hands by highly experienced workers. It is speculated that Khatamband was bought to Kashmir around the 14th century by the famous saint Shah-i-Hamdan, his followers also included Khatamband workers from Iran. These workers taught the art to the local artisans. Khatamband got popular due to its beauty and characteristic insulative qualities. As Kashmir experiences harsh, bitter winters, Khatamband was just the right thing. Later it is believed that Mirza Hyder Douglat worked like an ox of a man to spread this art throughout Kashmir. It is an expensive art and not many people invested in it for a long time, as a matter of fact, the mid-nineties saw the art gradually fading away owing to its expensive proposition.
