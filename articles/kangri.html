Kangri: The saviour of Kashmiris

What Laila was on Majnun’s bosom, so is the Kanger to a Kashmiri. Kanger(or kangri) is an earthen pot filled with hot embers woven around with wicker. It is usually used by Kashmiris beneath their traditional clothing called pheren to help keep the body warm during the chilly winter season. It is portable and lightweight, can be ignited with just 250 grams of charcoal, and provides a cheap alternative to the modern-day solution such as gas Bukharis and air conditioners. It is a Kashmiris best friend in winters because it is not unreliable like electricity. To this day Kangri remains an enduring emblem of local craft. So how exactly is a kangri made? First wicker sticks are harvested (just outskirts of Srinagar), then they are boiled to soften their skin, then the skins are peeled, the process is followed by drying the wicker sticks, at the same time potters make the earthen pot, then talented workers weave a network of wicker sticks to make beautiful, eye-catching kangris. But, to the dark side of our most loved kangri, regular use can cause a special type of skin cancer called kangri cancer! And no, I’m not joking.
